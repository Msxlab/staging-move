# Claude UX Judge — LocateFlow Experience Verdict

Date: 2026-06-15
Role: UX Judge + Product Experience Architect. Method: read the full `docs/ai/experience/*` scan + product-brain dashboard + experiment backlog, ran a 4-lens adversarial panel (Activation, Retention/Moat, Trust+Upgrade, Complexity Skeptic), then **independently verified the load-bearing source claims** before asserting them. No application source modified. No secrets/PII read.

> **Two verified facts the experience docs underweight — they change the whole UX answer:**
> 1. **Creating a moving plan is a paid unlock.** `/api/moving` POST runs `canCreateMovingPlan` → for free/trial users it returns **HTTP 403 "Upgrade to Individual to unlock your full move plan"** (`apps/web/src/lib/api-gates.ts:97`, `apps/web/src/app/api/moving/route.ts:83-86`, `api-gates.test.ts:115-120`). `FREE_TRIAL` is the default feature set (`workspace-entitlements.ts:56,59`). So the Command Center's countdown/readiness/checklist aha is **gated to Individual+**; the *free* first session is "organize your home" (addresses/providers/bills) + a **one-time** onboarding teaser.
> 2. **The AI Briefing is not the spine and cannot be made central.** The persistent, every-session, key-independent hero is the **deterministic Command Center + Next Critical Actions** (works with no Anthropic key, no paid plan). The Briefing **hides** under three independent conditions (key unconfigured/fetch-fails; FREE/INDIVIDUAL gate; no pending criticals), and on mobile it's a one-time-per-install "welcome card." A surface with three disappearance conditions cannot be the centerpiece.

> **Strict stance:** the scan is thorough but proposes ~5 *new* surfaces (transition board, monitoring surface, household mission-control screen, proof-packet surface, source-explainer) to fix fragmentation — which adds fragmentation. The Command Center is **the dashboard you already shipped**, trimmed to the two cards that matter, plus the moving-plan page as the work surface. Almost everything else should be a *view, filter, deep-link, or copy change* — not a new room.

---

## 1. Final UX north star

**Within one session and one screen, every household knows exactly what their move needs next — is never confused about what LocateFlow tracks vs. what it does — and keeps getting a reason to return after move day.**
(Supports the product north-star metric: *verified address transitions completed per active household*.)

## 2. Ideal first-session journey (one paragraph)

A new user accepts legal inline, enters household basics, types one address with autocomplete, and on the providers step taps **"Add all essentials" once** (already built) — the dashboard now has real data. They enter a destination + move date and immediately see the **personalized teaser**: "23 days to go, TX → NJ — here are your 5 most critical steps, and why," running the real `generateChecklist` engine. *This is the aha, and it should fire for every cohort, not only free-with-move.* From there: **paid** users land on a Command Center whose readiness ring moves when they complete one task via the `UpNext` one-tap; **free** users land on a dashboard led by **Next Critical Actions** (their free, keyless intelligence) with the move-plan countdown shown as a clearly-labeled Individual unlock and a blurred briefing teaser — so the free session still *demonstrates* intelligence and names exactly what upgrading buys, instead of going cold after one teaser.

## 3. Ideal 30-day user journey

- **Day 0 (active move):** Command Center hero (countdown + readiness ring + next action). Briefing is present (rule-based if AI off), enriching not gating. Transitions worked from the moving-plan page grouped into stop/start/transfer/update/cancel lanes.
- **Days 1–14:** Each completed task moves the readiness ring (micro-aha). Reminders fire only at real date windows. Household tasks assigned to members who can see their part.
- **Move day → +1:** Dashboard **reorders**: a single **"What's coming for this address"** card promotes to hero, stating the standing promise — "Move's done; from here I watch your bills, contracts, and renewals so nothing slips."
- **Days 15–30:** Quiet between real reminders (no manufactured pings). A weekly digest, framed as "N items / obligations coming up," is the re-engagement trigger that deep-links into the monitoring card. ~Day 14–21 the **moat aha**: a 30-day contract window surfaces a renewal the user forgot — "it caught it." Day 30: a recurring "what changed this month" summary (AI-enriched only if key set).

## 4. Top 5 experience principles

1. **One spine, two surfaces.** Dashboard ("where do I stand") + Moving-Plan page ("do the work") carry the whole Command Center. Everything else is a view, filter, deep-link, or copy change — never a new route.
2. **Deterministic spine, AI as bonus.** Comprehension and "what's next" must work with no key and no paid plan (Command Center, Next Critical Actions, readiness ring). The Briefing enriches; it must never gate comprehension or silently vanish.
3. **Honesty is the feature.** Anything that looks like automation must state, *at the point of action*, "LocateFlow tracks this — your provider account is unchanged." Trust copy is one reusable system, not scattered strings.
4. **Show value, then ask.** Upgrade prompts fire only at the verified moment of unmet need (plan creation, pending essentials, invite click, dossier PDF, tax export) — never a generic wall before value.
5. **Earn the return.** Value must outlive move day: one "what's coming for this address" surface, framed as money/obligations at stake, is the reason to come back.

## 5. Top 10 friction points to fix (ranked by activation/retention/revenue/trust risk)

1. **The aha is paywalled and the free first session goes cold.** Plan creation 403s for free; the one free aha (onboarding teaser) is one-shot and never re-surfaced. *(VERIFIED: moving/route.ts + api-gates.)* → Give free users a standing, keyless intelligence surface (Next Critical Actions promoted) and re-surface the teaser value.
2. **Point-of-action trust gap.** Marking a stop/start/transfer task complete shows the "LocateFlow only" guarantee *only* when `localEffect.localOnly` is set, as a tiny chip — otherwise no disclaimer. Highest support/refund/real-harm risk (user thinks the utility was switched). *(Panel-reported at `moving/plan/[id]/page.tsx:495`; consistent with verified "manual tracking only" posture.)*
3. **The centerpiece can silently vanish.** Briefing hides on keyless deploys / for free/individual / when no criticals — the rule-based fallback exists but the card *hides* instead of falling back. *(VERIFIED: fail-soft hide in briefing route + card.)*
4. **The strongest built aha reaches the narrowest audience.** Onboarding teaser fires only for `wantsToMove && !isPremium`; paid movers and skip-move users never see it. *(Panel-reported, source-grounded.)*
5. **Mobile briefing is one-time-per-install and hides when gated** (no teaser fallback like web). First-day dismissal = intelligence gone forever on that device. *(Panel-reported, consistent with verified mobile card.)*
6. **Dashboard is a 9–13 card wall.** The high-signal trio (Command Center, Next Critical Actions, briefing) competes with route map, budget donuts, sparklines. First impression reads "analytics dashboard," diluting "command center."
7. **Briefing has no provenance/disclaimer at first contact.** Rule-based vs AI is invisible; no "built from your address, services, and plan" line; no "informational, not advice" footer — inconsistent with the gold-standard dossier disclaimers.
8. **Post-move value is push-only and fragmented.** Reminders/contracts/lifecycle/bills split across notifications + digest + one buried `UpcomingBills` card; no pull destination = the episodic-move trap intact.
9. **Upgrade asks are mistimed, not dishonest.** Gates are honest and well-distributed, but most fire on generic dashboard/pricing surfaces rather than at the verified moment of unmet need.
10. **Landing connector copy implies on-your-behalf account changes** ("LocateFlow can submit your new address on your behalf") with the limiter trailing — the most externally-visible overpromise given no live account-change integrations are verified.

## 6. Top 10 aha moments to amplify (all existing primitives)

1. Personalized **onboarding teaser** countdown + top-5 critical steps (`buildMoveTeaser`/`generateChecklist`) — widen to all cohorts.
2. **Readiness ring climbs** when you complete one task via `UpNext` one-tap ("I did one thing and it moved").
3. **"Add all essentials" one-tap** in onboarding — chore → instant populated dashboard.
4. **Next Critical Actions** with "because" reasons + deadlines — briefing-grade intelligence that works with **no key and no paid plan**; the free-tier intelligence proof.
5. Briefing's **deep-linked next actions** (correct, category-preselected destinations) — amplify the rows, downplay the paragraph.
6. **Transition lanes** (a groupBy(actionType) view on the plan page): "here's everything to cancel / transfer / start."
7. **"It caught the renewal I forgot"** — a contract 30-day reminder surfacing a forgotten obligation (the core moat emotion).
8. **One "what's coming for this address" screen** turning scattered obligations into one ordered, dollar-anchored list.
9. **Proof on demand** — deep-link from a completed transition to the existing dossier/export PDF.
10. **Household assignment** — "everyone knows their part" via the existing assignee control + invitations.

## 7. Top 5 natural upgrade moments (each tied to a VERIFIED gate)

1. **Free user clicks "Start your move" / tries to create a plan → 403 "Upgrade to Individual to unlock your full move plan."** The single biggest, most natural conversion wall — it's the activation→paid boundary itself. Make the 403 render a value-framed CTA, not a bare error.
2. **Free/Individual with ≥1 pending essential → AI Briefing teaser (blurred preview) → Family/Pro** (`aiBriefing` gate). Natural because Next Critical Actions right above it already proves unmet need.
3. **User clicks "Invite household" / hits seat ceiling → "Upgrade to Family or Pro to invite members"** (`seats` gate; explicit intent).
4. **User viewing a Home Dossier wants the PDF / hits the locked Neighborhood Intelligence section → Pro** (`dossierPdf`/`neighborhoodIntel`). Tie the CTA to the dossier they're currently viewing.
5. **User on export/settings requests the tax/property report → Pro** (`advancedExport`); plus the **concurrent-plan 200 teaser** ("upgrade to Pro for multiple moves") for power users running a second move.

## 8. Top 5 metrics to instrument (coarse, consented, no-raw-PII)

1. **Activation funnel:** `onboarding_complete → first_address → essentials_added → teaser_viewed → first_meaningful_action` (plan opened OR task completed via UpNext OR service added) within session 1.
2. **North-star proxy:** `transition_completed` per active household (MoveTask → COMPLETED, proof yes/no) + `monitoring_item_resolved {type, outcome, lead_days_bucket}`.
3. **Briefing reliability + lift:** `briefing_state {ai|rule_based|teaser|hidden}` (catches the hide bug), `briefing_action_clicked`, action→transition_status_change (measures whether AI beats rule-based).
4. **Upgrade efficacy:** `upgrade_gate_viewed {surface: plan|briefing|seats|dossierPdf|advancedExport} → checkout_started → feature_used_after_upgrade` (which gate converts).
5. **Return/retention:** `return_session {days_since_move_bucket, trigger: digest|push|organic}` + D30 post-move return rate.
Allowed fields only: feature surface, tier bucket, provider category, 2-letter state, action type, status/outcome, blocker category, confidence bucket, proof yes/no. Never: raw address, account/confirmation numbers, names, emails, phone, amounts/dates as raw values, document contents.

## 9. Top 3 experiments to run first (sequenced risk → leverage → confidence)

1. **Command Center dependability + de-noise.** Make the briefing **always render** (use the existing rule-based fallback instead of hiding; add mobile teaser parity); pin Command Center + Next Critical Actions + briefing above reference widgets; demote route map / budget / dossier below the fold. *Prerequisite spine fix; key-independent; zero schema; reversible.*
2. **Point-of-action trust confirmation.** Make the "LocateFlow only — your provider account is unchanged" guarantee **unconditional** on stop/start/transfer/cancel completion (reuse `legal.ts` copy verbatim) + add briefing provenance + a one-line "informational, not advice" footer + fix the landing connector copy to lead with the limiter. *Fixes the asymmetric live-harm risk; copy + conditional-render only; reversible.*
3. **Widen the personalized onboarding teaser to all cohorts.** Show the engine-computed countdown + top-5 critical-steps screen to everyone who entered destination+date (not just `wantsToMove && !isPremium`), then route onward. *Highest-confidence activation lift; one-condition flip; instant rollback; key-independent.*

**Immediate fast-follow (#4): the post-move "What's coming for this address" monitoring hero** — the retention lever that beats the episodic-move trap (read-only re-aggregation of existing cron/Service date logic, promoted to hero when the active move is COMPLETED). Slightly higher build and a smaller near-term (paid, post-move) cohort, so it sequences right after the spine is reliable — but it is the most strategically important next surface.

**Rejected as experiment #1: "flip the AI key for a cohort A/B."** Do not A/B AI-vs-rule-based before the briefing reliably *renders*; fix the hide bug first (Experiment 1), then measure.
