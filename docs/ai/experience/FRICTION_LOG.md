# Friction Log

## Current verified experience

- LocateFlow has verified surfaces for onboarding, dashboard intelligence, AI Briefing, addresses, services, moving plans, move tasks, provider recommendations, exports, billing, household invitations, notifications, and post-move reminders.
- The strongest product direction is Address Life OS / Move Command Center, but current capability is distributed across several surfaces.
- No live customer analytics, support tickets, conversion reports, or usability research were inspected.

## User goal

Spot the product moments that may prevent a household from reaching the first clear value moment, completing transitions, trusting the system, or upgrading naturally.

## Emotional job

Reduce uncertainty for the user and the product team: know what is confusing, what is missing, and what should be fixed first.

## Current friction

1. The first "Move Command Center" value moment may be scattered across dashboard, moving, services, and onboarding.
2. AI Move Briefing exists, but may be treated as a dashboard card or mobile first-run moment rather than the central intelligence surface.
3. Provider Transition is supported by MoveTask data, but the verified UI is still closer to a task list than a transition workspace.
4. Post-move reminders exist, but are not packaged as a clear retention surface.
5. Proof/exports live in settings and dossier flows, away from the moments when users need proof.
6. Household collaboration exists through invitations and assignment, but lacks a verified mission-control summary.
7. Upgrade gates exist, but the natural paid moments need outcome-led framing.
8. Trust and limitation copy exists, but is distributed and must stay consistent as AI/provider experiences become more prominent.
9. Privacy-safe analytics are necessary, but live metrics were not verified in this scan.
10. SEO-first and partner-facing strategy remain deferred, so core product activation must carry learning.

## Aha opportunity

- Center the first session around one clear outcome: "Here is what your move needs next."
- Make AI Briefing the visible intelligence layer, not a hidden or one-time feature.
- Turn MoveTask action types into provider-transition lanes.
- Package post-move monitoring as "nothing slips after move day."
- Turn exports into move proof packets tied to completed transitions.

## Revenue opportunity

- Convert at moments of urgent value: AI next actions, proof packet, blocked provider transition, household invite, and post-move obligation monitoring.
- Reduce refund/support risk with precise claims and trust copy.
- Use Family/Pro packaging around jobs users already understand.

## Data moat opportunity

- Convert friction into consented, coarse transition-outcome learning: blocker categories, action types, statuses, proof generation, monitoring resolution, and household task ownership.
- Avoid treating unverified customer behavior as evidence.

## Hypotheses

- Hypothesis: a clearer first-session path to AI Briefing will improve activation.
- Hypothesis: a transition board will make existing MoveTask data feel more valuable than a checklist.
- Hypothesis: proof packets and monitoring will create stronger paid intent than generic plan descriptions.

## Recommended experiments

1. AI Briefing visibility/source-explainer experiment.
2. Provider Transition board read-only experiment.
3. Post-move monitoring surface experiment.
4. Proof-packet preview experiment.
5. Household invite-at-assignment experiment.
