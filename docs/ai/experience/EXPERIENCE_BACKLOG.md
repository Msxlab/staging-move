# Experience Backlog

## Current verified experience

- Verified primitives exist for AI Briefing, move tasks, task assignment, service records, provider recommendations, exports, dossiers, notifications, subscription gates, household invitations, and mobile/web onboarding.
- The UX opportunity is mainly surfacing, sequencing, packaging, and measuring these primitives rather than inventing new product areas.
- No application source code was modified to create this backlog.

## User goal

Prioritize experience work that helps a household feel immediate value, complete provider transitions, stay protected after move day, and upgrade for clear outcomes.

## Emotional job

Make LocateFlow feel like a calm command center instead of a collection of useful but separate tools.

## Current friction

- The highest-value surfaces exist, but their story is not yet unified around the Address Life OS / Move Command Center.
- Several likely paid moments are verified as capability but not yet packaged as user-facing outcomes.
- Instrumentation and customer evidence must be added before hypotheses become product facts.

## Aha opportunity

1. AI Move Briefing: "It knows what my move needs next."
2. Provider Transition Workspace: "Every provider has a lane, status, owner, and next step."
3. Post-Move Monitoring: "It keeps watching the stuff I would forget after move day."
4. Proof Packet: "I can produce a credible move record when someone asks."
5. Household Mission Control: "Everyone knows their part."

## Revenue opportunity

1. AI Briefing paid intelligence preview.
2. Proof packet Pro export.
3. Provider Transition Workspace for move operations.
4. Post-move monitoring retention surface.
5. Family household collaboration.

## Data moat opportunity

1. Transition action type and outcome.
2. Blocker category and resolution.
3. Proof generated yes/no by packet type.
4. Monitoring obligation resolved yes/no.
5. Household assignment and completion by role bucket.

## Hypotheses

- Hypothesis: the first three source-code experiments should be AI Briefing experience hardening, Provider Transition board, and Post-Move Monitoring surface.
- Hypothesis: proof packet preview is the fastest Pro willingness-to-pay test after briefing hardening.
- Hypothesis: household collaboration is both an activation feature and a growth loop once task assignment is prominent.

## Recommended experiments

1. AI Briefing visibility, source, and upgrade teaser.
2. Provider Transition board read-only v1 over existing MoveTask data.
3. Post-move monitoring surface over existing bill, contract, lifecycle, and digest primitives.
4. Proof packet preview and Pro upgrade teaser.
5. Household invite prompt at task assignment.
6. Onboarding fastest-path to first AI Briefing.
7. Trust-copy consolidation across provider, AI, export, and pricing surfaces.
8. Pricing moment matrix with outcome-led copy.
9. Privacy-safe event taxonomy.
10. Address Timeline preview using existing address/service/move history.
