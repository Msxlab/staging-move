# Current verified product capability

- LocateFlow currently supports account/profile, addresses, services, moving plans, move tasks, budgets, workspaces, providers, recommendations, exports, notifications, tickets, subscriptions, and mobile access.
- The Claude Product Strategy Council selected the initial customer segment as the household move-organizer running a multi-provider and/or state-to-state move.
- Verified technical capability supports this segment through workspaces, assignments, move tasks, services, budgets, provider recommendations, exports, subscriptions, and mobile access.
- No customer interviews, support logs, conversion data, cohort analysis, or willingness-to-pay data were verified.

# Hypotheses

- Hypothesis: the core emotional job is "make sure I do not miss anything expensive, legal, or annoying during my move."
- Hypothesis: the functional jobs are remembering every address-linked obligation, deciding what to switch or cancel, coordinating household responsibilities, producing proof, and tracking provider status.
- Hypothesis: the social job is being able to show a spouse, roommate, landlord, insurer, mover, school, employer, or property manager that the move is under control.
- Hypothesis: customers with more providers, children, vehicles, cross-state moves, or complex billing/contract obligations will have higher willingness to pay.
- Hypothesis: the single payer is usually the move-owner, not the invited household member.
- Hypothesis: a household workspace can become a recurring Address Life OS product if it remains useful after move day.

# Recommended next decisions

- Accepted initial customer segment: the household move-organizer running a multi-provider and/or state-to-state move.
- Accepted primary buyer: the single move-owner who owns the workspace, service list, budget pressure, and transition accountability.
- Accepted top jobs-to-be-done:
  - Know exactly what still needs action before, during, and after move day.
  - Stop, start, transfer, cancel, or compare providers without losing track.
  - Assign transition work to household members while retaining accountability.
  - Produce proof for landlord, insurance, household, or personal records.
  - Avoid post-move billing surprises, contract renewals, and forgotten obligations.
- Do not anchor the first revenue strategy on secondary household members, B2B partners, or live provider integrations.
