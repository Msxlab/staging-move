# Current verified product capability

- Verified current capabilities include public content/catalog surfaces such as help, blog, providers, movers, mover applications, provider catalog flows, recommendations, exports, subscriptions, and mobile access.
- Admin memory includes blog, providers, movers, acquisition campaigns, sponsored placements, affiliate exports/conversions, reports, logs, and runtime config.
- The Claude Product Strategy Council selected household invites and transition assignments as the first growth loop.
- No traffic, SEO ranking, conversion, ad spend, campaign performance, or customer acquisition data was inspected.
- The current system appears technically capable of supporting content-led and tool-led acquisition, but growth performance is unverified.

# Hypotheses

- Hypothesis: the best first growth motion is household invites to assigned transition tasks, because invited members enter the product mid-move with a concrete job.
- Hypothesis: a meaningful fraction of invited partners, roommates, adult children, or household collaborators may become future move-owners.
- Hypothesis: exports/dossiers/proof packets can become shareable artifacts that pull landlords, co-tenants, or household members into the workflow.
- Hypothesis: SEO free tools can become a feeder later after account-save artifacts and rules review exist.
- Hypothesis: partner channels such as movers, property managers, relocation services, and providers may become growth levers only after verified agreements.

# Recommended next decisions

- Accepted growth loop: household invites -> transition assignments -> second-member activation -> next move-owner.
- Use existing workspace invitations, task assignment, seat limits, exports/dossiers, and transition board work as the first growth primitives.
- Treat SEO free tools as a fast-follow feeder, not the first growth engine.
- Defer partner channel growth until partnerships and distribution are explicitly verified.
- Establish growth guardrails: no fake partner logos, no invented reviews, no unverified integration claims, no customer-data claims.

- What landing page promises are safe before growth metrics exist?
